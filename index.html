Current Position

Jul 2016 - PresentAssociate Research Fellow
Ingham Group, Living Systems Institute, University of Exeter
Key projects:"Evolution of the Hedgehog signalling pathway."

Hedgehog signalling is cruical for the development of most animals although the mechanism has diverged between vertebrate and invertebrates.

I am developing a Nematostella vectensis model for the mechanistic and functional study of Hedgehog pathway components. I am characterising the Hedgehog pathway and loss of function phenotype in Nematotella and generating Nematostella genetic mutants using CRISPR/Cas9 to study the role of Cos2/Kif7, Fused, and the requirement for ciliary-mediated signal transduction.

Jan 2017 - Mar 2017Postdoctoral Research Assistant
Smith Group, The Francis Crick Institute, Mill Hill Laboratory
Key projects:"Comparison of tmem88a mutants and morpholino knockdown phenotypes."

Tmem88a is believed to be a negative regulator of the Wnt signalling pathway, required for cardiogenesis and primitive haematopoiesis, although studies have obtained inconsistent results using morpholino oligonucleotides.

I generated an 8 bp deletion in the coding region of the tmem88a locus using TALENs, and established a viable homozygous tmem88aΔ8 mutant line. Although tmem88aΔ8 mutants have reduced expression of some key haematopoietic genes, differentiation of primitive haematopoiesis is unaffected. I found that expression of the tmem88a paralogue tmem88b is not significantly changed in tmem88aΔ8 mutants and injection of the tmem88a splice-blocking morpholino oligonucleotide into tmem88aΔ8 mutants causes the reduction of erythrocytes observed in morphants. This suggested that there is a partial, but inessential, requirement for tmem88a during haematopoiesis and that nonspecific off target effects of morpholino injection exacerbates this phenotype in tmem88a knockdown embryos. (Eve et al. 2017)

"Investigating the role of lamc3 during zebrafish development."

Laminins are a major component of the basement membrane. A number of zebrafish mutants exist for different laminin chains, however there has been no functional study of laminin γ3.

In situ and transcriptome data suggests that lamc3 is expressed in the vasculature of developing zebrafish. Knockdown using morpholino oligonucleotides prevents neural migration and development of the lymphatic system, although with possible off target effects. Future work will aim to generate a lamc3 genetic mutant for further functional study.

Jan 2017 - Mar 2017PhD Student
Smith Group, The Francis Crick Institute, Mill Hill Laboratory
Thesis:"Identification and functional analyses of novel genes in haemovasculogensis".

I used previously generated RNA-sequencing data to functionally characterise novel genes with roles in haemovascular development (Cannon et al. 2013). Genes enriched in endothelial cells were knocked down using morpholino oligonucleotides and phenotypes were characterised in transgenic zebrafish lines. Two genes of interest, tmem88a and lamc3, were selected for further study.
